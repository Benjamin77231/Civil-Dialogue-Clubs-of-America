About Us

What We Provide

Two Methods Toward One Mission

CDCA's work has two distinct aims, and both are essential to the same larger goal.

01
🏫
Pillar One

Club Expansion

We provide high school students with everything they need to start a CDCA-affiliated politics club at their school.

This means a formal Starter Kit, facilitation training, and ongoing support from the CDCA network. A CDCA club isn't just another student organization; it's a commitment to a specific kind of community that embraces curiosity, findind common ground, and challenging biases.

Pillar Two

Leader Training

Thousands of students already lead political discussion clubs at their schools, but most were never taught how to facilitate a truly productive, civil discussion.

CDCA's training program addresses that gap directly. We train student leaders in the specific skills that make hard conversations productive.

Core Values

What every CDCA club stands for

⚖️

Strict Neutrality

CDCA holds no political positions; our clubs are forums, not platforms. Every viewpoint is welcome at the table.

🗣️

Civil Discourse

We believe disagreement is not only healthy, but necessary. However, civility is the condition that makes meaningful disagreement possible.

🙋

Every Voice Matters

We believe that every student has a valuable perspective to contribute. Though we never pressure anyone to participate, we encourage all members to share their thoughts.

🎯

Student-Led

The best civic education is self-governed. CDCA never takes over a club — we equip students to run them. That means trusting young people with real responsibility, because that trust is itself a form of civic development.

🔍

Intellectual Honesty

We ask our members to engage with the strongest version of the arguments they disagree with. While strawmanning is easy, Steelmanning is where growth happens.

🌱

Long-Term Thinking

Meetings will not be perfect at first, and there will most certainly be challenges. However, we're trying to build a generation that knows how to talk across difference, and we're willing to do the slow work that requires.

Meet the Founder

The Story Behind CDCA

Founder & Executive Director, CDCA

I'm a student at The West End Secondary Public School in New York City with a deep frustration and a deeper belief — frustrated by how divided this country has become, and convinced that young people can actually do something about it.

It started small. I began creating informal political discussion spaces among my friends, many of whom had little interest in politics and even less awareness of what was happening around them. I wasn't trying to change their minds; instead, I was trying to open them up to different perspectives. Eventually, I realised that I could make an official forum for this kind of dialogue, so I created my school's first-ever politics club.

We built a community of young people across the political spectrum who came together, listened, and learned from each other. I thought, "Other schools should have communities like this too!"

That thought became the CDCA, a youth civic organization I founded to bring this model to high schools across America. The focus on high school is intentional. It's where political identities begin to take root, which means that we need to do everything in our power to challenge viewpoints and assumptions. If we can reach students at that moment, not to tell them what to think but to teach them how to think across difference, we have a real shot at changing the culture of political conversation in this country.
