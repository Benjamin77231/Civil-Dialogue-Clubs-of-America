What We Believe

The principles behind every club

Civil dialogue isn't a soft skill — it's a civic skill. These four principles guide every club we support and every leader we train.

⚖️

Strict Neutrality

No party line, no agenda. CDCA clubs hold no institutional positions — they are forums for every view, not platforms for any one.

🗣️

Civil Discourse First

Disagreement is healthy. Disrespect is not. Every CDCA club commits to a standard of engagement where people challenge ideas, not each other's dignity.

🙋

Every Voice Matters

A room where only the loudest voices speak is not a dialogue. We build structured formats that ensure every student — regardless of confidence or politics — gets a real seat at the table.

🎯

Student-Led, Always

These clubs belong to students. CDCA provides the framework; students provide the energy, the leadership, and the ideas. We don't run clubs — we empower the people who do.

How It Started

Built by a student, for students

CDCA grew out of a single experiment: a college student who believed his campus needed a space where liberals and conservatives could actually talk — not debate to win, but discuss to understand.

The club worked. Students showed up from across the political spectrum. Real conversations happened. Friendships formed across lines that had seemed uncrossable. And the model became something worth sharing.

Now CDCA is bringing that model to high schools across America — where the habits of democratic citizenship are first formed.
