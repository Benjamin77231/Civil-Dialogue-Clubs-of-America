Leader Training

Already running a political or civics club? Learn to run it better — more civilly, more inclusively, and more effectively.

Who This Is For

Good intentions aren't enough. Skills are learnable.

Thousands of high school students across America lead political or civics clubs. They're motivated, engaged, and care about their communities. But most were never taught how to actually run a productive political discussion.

How do you keep a conversation from turning into a shouting match? How do you make sure the quiet student in the back gets to speak? How do you stay neutral as a facilitator when you have your own strong opinions? How do you manage the one person who dominates every discussion?

These are skills. They can be taught. That's what CDCA's Leader Training program exists to do.

This training is for students who already lead a club — not for students looking to start one. If you're starting from scratch, see our Start a Club page instead.

The Curriculum

What you'll learn

CDCA's training program is structured around the specific challenges of running civil political discussions in a high school setting. These are not generic leadership skills — they are specific facilitation skills for a specific context.

01
🧭

The Neutral Facilitator

How to lead a discussion without showing your own hand — and why your neutrality as a leader is the precondition for everyone else's freedom to speak honestly.

  • Reading your own biases before a session
  • Language that stays neutral under pressure
  • What to do when someone asks what you personally think
  • Managing tone, body language, and facial reactions
02
🗂️

Discussion Design

A great discussion doesn't happen by accident. Learn to choose and adapt formats that generate genuine dialogue rather than rehearsed talking points.

  • Socratic seminars, fishbowls, and structured debates
  • Question design that opens minds rather than closes them
  • How to sequence a discussion across 60–90 minutes
  • Choosing topics that are interesting without being explosive
03
🌡️

Managing Conflict

Disagreement is healthy. Escalation is not. Learn to distinguish productive tension from destructive conflict — and how to de-escalate without shutting down real debate.

  • The difference between debate and argument
  • Specific language for de-escalating heated moments
  • How to address personal attacks without taking sides
  • When to pause, redirect, or end a line of discussion
04
🙋

Inclusive Spaces

A room where only the loudest voices speak isn't a dialogue. Learn to build structures that give everyone — particularly quieter or more marginalized students — a real voice.

  • Managing dominant personalities without embarrassing them
  • Drawing out reluctant participants
  • Addressing moments when identities, not just ideas, feel attacked
  • Pair and small-group formats that lower barriers to participation
05
📐

Ground Rules That Work

Community agreements only work if students actually believe in them. Learn to establish, reinforce, and revisit ground rules in ways that build genuine buy-in — not just compliance.

  • Co-creating norms with your group vs. imposing them
  • What to do when a ground rule is violated mid-discussion
  • Revisiting and updating norms as your club evolves
  • The CDCA Community Agreement framework
06
📈

Building Sustainable Culture

One great meeting doesn't build a club. Learn to create the culture, traditions, and leadership pipeline that keeps a club alive and growing beyond any single leader.

  • Running effective leadership transitions
  • Building rituals that reinforce your club's values
  • Handling burnout — yours and your members'
  • Measuring success when the goal is dialogue, not outcomes
How It Works

The training format

💻

Virtual Sessions

Training is delivered virtually via video call, making it accessible to student leaders across the country regardless of geography. Sessions are recorded for review.

📅

Cohort-Based

Participants go through training in small cohorts of 6–12 student leaders. Peer learning and cross-school connection are built into the format intentionally.

⏱️

6-Week Commitment

Training runs over six weeks, with one 90-minute session per week. Between sessions, participants complete short reflection exercises and apply skills at their own clubs.

🛠️

Practice-Heavy

This isn't a lecture series. Each session includes structured practice scenarios, role-playing difficult facilitation moments, and peer feedback. Skills develop by doing.

📚

Take-Home Materials

Every session comes with a resource packet you keep: summaries, frameworks, scripts, and checklists you can use directly in your own club meetings.

🎓

Certificate of Completion

Participants who complete all six sessions receive a CDCA Certificate of Completion in Civil Dialogue Facilitation — a meaningful addition to any college application.
