Start a Club

Launch a nonpartisan politics club at your high school — and get everything you need to do it well from day one.

Who This Is For

If you believe in honest conversation, this is for you.

You don't need to be a political science major. You don't need prior experience running a club. You just need to believe that your school deserves a space where students can engage with the real questions of political life — without it turning into a shouting match.

CDCA-affiliated clubs are open to students of every political background. They don't take positions, don't endorse candidates, and don't exist to push any agenda. They exist to practice the hardest civic skill there is: listening to someone you fundamentally disagree with and engaging with their ideas honestly.

If that sounds like something your school is missing — let's build it together.

Good fit if your school...

  • ✅ Doesn't have a nonpartisan politics or current events club
  • ✅ Has a politically diverse student body
  • ✅ Has even one teacher willing to serve as faculty advisor
  • ✅ Has a student willing to commit to leading the first semester

What you don't need...

  • 🚫 Prior experience running a club or organization
  • 🚫 Any particular political viewpoint to start
  • 🚫 Funding — the Starter Kit is provided free to qualifying schools
  • 🚫 A perfectly formed plan — we'll help you build it
The Path Forward

How to launch a CDCA club at your school

1

Express Your Interest

Fill out the interest form below. This is not a formal application, we just want to get to know you and your school better.

2

Connect with CDCA

A member of the CDCA team will reach out to you directly within 1-3 business days. We'll set up a short call to discuss further details.

3

Receive Your Starter Kit

You'll receive the full CDCA Starter Kit, which you can learn all about below. It's everything you need to build a strong foundation for your club.

4

Launch Your First Meeting

Use our excellent recruitment materials to spread the word, and our First Meeting Guide to run a session that makes people want to come back. We'll be available to debrief with you afterward.

5

You're in the CDCA Network!

Your club is now part of a national network of schools committed to civil dialogue. You'll have access to ongoing support, all of our materials, and a community of student leaders doing the same work.

What You'll Receive

The CDCA Starter Kit

We designed the Starter Kit so that a motivated high schooler with no prior experience can launch a strong, sustainable club — without reinventing the wheel.

📋

Club Charter Template

A founding document that establishes your club's nonpartisan mission, membership rules, officer structure, and decision-making process. Approved by school administrators in every club we've worked with.

🗓️

Meeting Format Library

Structured formats for Socratic seminars, fishbowl debates, "steel-manning" exercises, and moderated issue discussions. Designed for high school groups of 8–40 students.

🤝

Facilitator's Guide

Step-by-step scripts and tips for running your first five meetings, including how to open discussions, handle difficult moments, and close sessions productively.

📣

Recruitment Materials

Customizable flyers, social media graphics, an announcement script for teachers, and a school club fair pitch guide. Everything you need to fill seats.

📊

Balanced Issue Briefings

A library of one-page briefings on major policy topics — each presenting multiple perspectives with equal rigor. No slant, no spin. A real foundation for informed discussion.

📁

Faculty Advisor Guide

A guide specifically for the teacher or administrator who agrees to advise your club — including their role, expectations, and how to support without taking over.

📝

Ground Rules Framework

Our tested set of community agreements for civil discussion — adaptable for your club's specific needs and formatted to hang on the wall of your meeting space.

🌐

CDCA Network Access

Connect with other CDCA club leaders, get ongoing support, access new materials as they're released, and participate in national dialogue events as the network grows.

Common Questions

Things students ask us

Does my school need to be a certain size?

No. We've seen successful clubs at schools with 200 students and schools with 3,000. What matters is having at least one motivated leader and a teacher willing to advise. Even a small founding group of 8–10 students is enough to start well.

What if my school already has a political club?

That's fine — CDCA clubs serve a different purpose than Young Democrats or Young Republicans chapters. Nonpartisan discussion clubs coexist well with partisan organizations, and sometimes draw members from both. If you already lead an existing club and want training, check out our Leader Training program instead.

Is the Starter Kit really free?

Yes. All digital Starter Kit materials are provided at no cost to qualifying schools. CDCA's mission is expansion, not revenue — we want to remove every barrier we can between a motivated student and a functional club.

How much time does running a club take?

Most CDCA clubs meet once a month to once every two weeks. A well-run monthly meeting takes about 1–2 hours of preparation from the lead facilitator, plus the meeting itself. The Starter Kit is designed to minimize prep time through reusable formats and pre-built materials.

What if things go sideways at a meeting?

It happens. We'll prepare you for it. The Facilitator's Guide includes specific guidance for difficult moments — and you'll have access to CDCA team support if you need a debrief after a hard session. The goal is not to prevent all conflict. It's to navigate conflict constructively.
